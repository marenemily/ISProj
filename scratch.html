My Violin Adventures

My Story

  • I have played the violin since I was 8 years old. I hated it for the longest time, but when I joined my middle school orchestra, I grew to love it!
  • Although I loved it, I didn't see myself doing this for the rest of my life. So when I got to high school, I joined the program for one year to get my credit, and then planned to quit.
  • When my orchestra director, Ms. Prontka, saw that I wasn't on her role for the next year, she pulled me into her office and asked me what was going on. I told her I wanted to quit. She said, "no, you're not going to quit." She then asked why, and I said I wanted a study hall, and she said, "no, that's just not good enough."
  • I quickly folded and changed my schedule. Partially because I was terrified of this woman, but also because I knew she was right. I ended up staying in her program all four years of high school and it was the best part of my high school experience.
  • Orchestra harbored my passion for music and no matter what else was happening in my life, I always knew I had my 55 minute class to take a break and just play my violin. It became my therapy. It also helped my leadership skills - learning how to lead my section and orchestra as first chair, managing the class as Vice President, etc.
  • I am eternally grateful for Ms. Prontka, my violin, and music.

My favorite pieces and why:

  1. Adventures on Bainbridge Island by Alan Lee Silva
    • Dynamics
    • The changing melodies between the different instruments
    • Played this at our Winter Concert when I was 16 and I still have it memorized
  2. La La Land
    • Iconic movie music, one of my favorite movies
    • Hard rythmns, but fun to learn
    • Played this at my senior concert, it was a dream come true!
  3. La Valse de L'Amour by Patrick Doyle
    • The song that Cinderella and the Prince first dance to at the ball - Live Action Cinderella 2015
    • Fell in love with this song in 3rd grade, perfectly captured the magic of this scene
    • I love the how the violin part keeps getting higher and higher
  4. Hymne a L'amour by Marguerite Monnot
    • I heard it for the first time while watching the Olympic opening ceremony in 2024 - Celine Dion on top of the Eiffel Tower singing through her condition had me in tears
    • The music itself if very simple and easy to learn, but still so beautiful and full of emotion
